Pollution Control Association of the Philippines Inc.

This organization promoted cleaner production, waste reduction, and pollution prevention in all sectors of society and industry. It is committed to becoming an active partner in institutionalizing environmental preservation, continual improvement, compliance with regulations, and ensuring that Filipino citizens have a sustainable future.

Greenpeace

The cause for establishing this organization was because of the nuclear tests that America did in the 60's and 70’s. The first unofficial action of the said organization took place on October 16, 1970, in Vancouver as a protest against a nuclear test of ever more powerful bombs. On September 15,1971, eco-activists sent a ship to Alaska in order to stop dangerous tests in a seismically dangerous region. The principles of Greenpeace are, Protests through action and not violence, because they believe that it brings positive results and can inspire people to change their attitude towards the environment.

Greenpeace >
Foundation for the Philippine Environment

Foundation for the Philippine Environment (FPE) works to encourage action and change for the protection of both nature and people. Its goal is to grow an organization and to promote environmentally responsible behaviors and policies in order to achieve a clean, healthy, and sustainable environment. This organization, which was created in January 1992, is presently the largest grant-making environmental institution beyond the Philippine government.

FPE >
Ecological Society of the Philippines

This organization also strives to maintain biodiversity and raise awareness about the necessity of protecting the ocean from pollution. It also aims to examine the country's environmental problems and develop scientific-based solutions.

ESP (FB Page) >
iVolunteer Philippines

It is a platform and group that provides assistance and support to the country in many difficulties, one of which is dealing with environmental issues. iVolunteer Philippines promotes and organizes environmental volunteering events such as tree planting, clean-up drives, and sustainable living programs. The organization was created in 2009 in response to the devastation caused by Typhoon Ondoy.

iVPH >
World Wildlife Fund for Nature

WWF works to help local communities conserve the natural resources they depend upon; transform markets and policies toward sustainability; and protect and restore species and their habitats. WWF ensures that the value of nature is reflected in decision-making from a local to a global scale.

WWF >
United Nations Environmental Programme

UNEP’s mission is to provide leadership and encourage partnership in caring for the environment by inspiring, informing, and enabling nations and peoples to improve their quality of life without compromising that of future generations. UNEP works on delivering transformational change for people and nature by drilling down on the root causes of the three planetary crises of climate change, nature and biodiversity loss, and pollution and waste.
